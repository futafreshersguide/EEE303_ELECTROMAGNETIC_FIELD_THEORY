📝 The "Full Marks" Protocol

FUTA EEE lecturers prioritize Process over the final number. To secure maximum marks in theory:

  • Cite the Principle: Always start with "By Gauss's Law..." or "Using Stokes' Theorem..."
  • Standard Units: Never omit V/m, C/m², or A/m. A missing unit can cost 0.5 marks.
  • The Sketch Advantage: Draw the Gaussian Surface or the coordinate planes. It proves you aren't just memorizing formulas.

🔄 Integration Mastery

Expect triple integrals. Master the differential volumes (dv):
• Cylindrical: ρ dρ dφ dz
• Spherical: r² sinθ dr dθ dφ

📐 Vector Transformation

Direct substitution of coordinates is a trap. You must apply the Dot Product Matrix to transform the unit vectors themselves (ax, ay to aρ, aφ).

📘 STEP-BY-STEP EXAM SOLUTIONS

Q1 · Coordinate Transformation (Point & Vector)
Given point P(-2, 6, 3) and vector A = y ax + (x+z) ay. Express P and A in cylindrical coordinates and evaluate A at P.
Step 1: Point P to (ρ, φ, z)
ρ = √((-2)² + 6²) = √40 ≈ 6.32 | φ = tan⁻¹(6/-2) = 108.43° (QII) | z = 3.
Step 2: Evaluate Cartesian Components at P
Ax = y = 6 | Ay = x+z = -2+3 = 1.
Step 3: Transform Vector using Matrix
Aρ = 6cos(108.43°) + 1sin(108.43°) = -0.95
Aφ = -6sin(108.43°) + 1cos(108.43°) = -6.01
A(cyl) = -0.95aρ - 6.01aφ
Q2 · Electrostatic Vector Force
Q₁=50μC at (-1, 1, -3) and Q₂=10μC at (3, 1, 0). Find vector force ON Q₁.
⚠️ TRICK: Force ON Q₁ requires vector FROM Q₂ TO Q₁ (R₂₁).
Step 1: R vector & Magnitude
R₂₁ = (-1-3)ax + (1-1)ay + (-3-0)az = -4ax - 3az. | |R| = 5m.
Step 2: Coulomb's Calculation
F = [ (9e9)(50e-6)(10e-6) / 5² ] * (-0.8ax - 0.6az) = 0.18 * (-0.8ax - 0.6az).
F = -0.144ax - 0.108az N (|F| = 0.18N)
