Exploratory Notes & Essential Formulas

Electrostatics deals with charges that are stationary ($v = 0$). This means there are no magnetic fields generated by these charges, only electric fields. http://googleusercontent.com/image_content/295

1. Coulomb's Law & Electric Field Intensity (E)
  • Coulomb's Law: The force between two point charges is directly proportional to the product of the charges and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them. F = (Q_1 Q_2) / (4πε_o R^2) a_R
  • Electric Field Intensity (E): The force per unit charge. Measured in Volts/meter (V/m). E = F / Q = Q / (4πε_o R^2) a_R
2. Standard Charge Distributions (MEMORIZE THESE)

Sadiku loves testing you on infinite charge configurations. Do not waste time deriving them in the exam; know the end formulas.

  • Infinite Line Charge (ρ_L): The field radiates outward in a cylinder. It decreases as $1/\rho$ (NOT $1/r^2$). E = ρ_L / (2πε_o ρ) a_ρ
  • Infinite Sheet of Charge (ρ_S): The field is uniform everywhere! It does NOT decrease with distance. E = ρ_S / (2ε_o) a_n
3. Electric Flux Density (D) & Gauss's Law

Electric flux density ($D$) is independent of the medium (permittivity $\epsilon$), unlike $E$ which gets weaker in a dielectric. http://googleusercontent.com/image_content/323

  • Relation: D = ε E = ε_o ε_r E
  • Gauss's Law (Integral Form): The total outward flux through a closed surface equals the total charge enclosed. ∮ D · dS = Q_enc = ∫ ρ_v dv
  • Gauss's Law (Differential Form): The divergence of D equals the volume charge density at that point. ∇ · D = ρ_v
4. Electric Potential (V) & Work Done

The electrostatic field is conservative. Moving a charge from point A to point B does not depend on the path taken.

  • Work Done: W = -Q ∫ E · dl = Q(V_B - V_A)
  • Maxwell's Equation for E: Because the field is conservative, the work done in a closed loop is zero. ∮ E · dl = 0 (Integral Form)
    ∇ × E = 0 (Differential Form → The field is irrotational)
  • E and V Relation: E = -∇V
⚠ O'YEMI'S BOUNDARY CONDITION TRAPS:
When an electric field crosses the boundary between two different media:
1. The Tangential component of E is continuous: $E_{t1} = E_{t2}$.
2. The Normal component of D is discontinuous if there is a surface charge: $D_{n1} - D_{n2} = \rho_S$. (If no surface charge, $D_{n1} = D_{n2}$).
3. Inside a perfect conductor, $E = 0$ and $\rho_v = 0$. All charge resides strictly on the surface!
