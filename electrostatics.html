Exploratory Notes & Essential Formulas

Electrostatics is the study of electric charges at rest ($v = 0$). Because the charges are stationary, they do not produce magnetic fields. The entire module is built on two foundational pillars: Coulomb's Law (for finding forces) and Gauss's Law (for exploiting symmetry).

1. Coulomb's Law & Electric Field Intensity (E)

Coulomb's Law states that the force between two point charges is directly proportional to the product of the charges and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them.

http://googleusercontent.com/image_content/223
  • Electrostatic Force (F): F = (Q1 Q2) / (4πεo R2) aR
  • Electric Field Intensity (E): Defined as the force experienced per unit positive test charge. Its unit is Volts/meter (V/m). E = F / Q = Q / (4πεo R2) aR
2. Standard Charge Distributions (MEMORIZE THESE)

In the exam, Sadiku will test you on infinite continuous charge distributions. Do not waste time deriving them from scratch in the hall; memorize the final formulas!

http://googleusercontent.com/image_content/237
  • Infinite Line Charge (ρL): Imagine a long, charged wire. The field radiates outward in a cylindrical pattern. Notice it decreases as 1/ρ, not 1/r²! E = ρL / (2πεo ρ) aρ
  • Infinite Sheet of Charge (ρS): Imagine a massive flat plane of charge. The electric field is completely uniform. It does not depend on how far away you are from the sheet! E = ρS / (2εo) an
3. Electric Flux Density (D) & Gauss's Law

Electric Field Intensity (E) gets weaker when it passes through different materials (dielectrics). To solve this, we define Electric Flux Density (D), which depends ONLY on the source charge and ignores the material medium.

  • The Relationship: D = ε E = εo εr E
  • Gauss's Law (Integral Form): The total outward flux passing through a completely closed imaginary surface (Gaussian surface) is exactly equal to the total net charge enclosed within it. ∮ D · dS = Qenc = ∫ ρv dv
  • Gauss's Law (Differential Form): Applying the Divergence Theorem gives us Maxwell's First Equation for Electrostatics. It proves that charge density is the source of electric flux. ∇ · D = ρv
4. Electric Potential (V) & Maxwell's Second Law

Because electrostatic fields are created by stationary charges, they are conservative. The work done moving a charge from point A to point B does not depend on the path taken. If you move a charge in a complete circle back to where it started, the net work done is ZERO.

http://googleusercontent.com/image_content/275
  • Electric Potential Difference: The work done per unit charge. VAB = - ∫ E · dl
  • Relationship to E: The electric field always points in the direction of the maximum rate of decrease of potential. E = -∇V
  • Maxwell's Equation for E: Because the field is conservative, it cannot "swirl" or form closed loops. It is strictly irrotational.
    Integral Form: ∮ E · dl = 0
    Differential Form: ∇ × E = 0
5. Boundary Conditions (The Exam Favorite)

What happens when an electric field crosses from Air into Glass? The field bends! You must know the rules of this boundary.

  • Tangential Components: The tangential component of the Electric Field Intensity (E) is always continuous across a boundary. Et1 = Et2
  • Normal Components: The normal component of the Electric Flux Density (D) is continuous ONLY if there is no free surface charge (ρS = 0) exactly on the boundary. Dn1 - Dn2 = ρS
  • Inside a Perfect Conductor: E = 0 and ρv = 0. All charges instantly rush to the outer surface. Just outside the conductor, the E-field is always 100% perpendicular (normal) to the surface.
⚠ O'YEMI'S LAPLACE TRAP:
If a question asks you to find the potential $V$ in an "empty space" or a "charge-free region" between two capacitor plates, you must use Laplace's Equation.

Poisson's Equation: ∇²V = -ρv / ε
If the region is charge-free ($\rho_v = 0$), Poisson's equation beautifully simplifies to Laplace's Equation: ∇²V = 0.

Remember: Energy density in an electric field is proportional to the square of the electric field! ($W_E = \frac{1}{2}\epsilon E^2$).
