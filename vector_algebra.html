Survival Hints & Lecture Notes

1. Scalars vs. Vectors
  • Scalar: Has only magnitude (e.g., Time, Mass, Distance, Electric Potential).
  • Vector: Has magnitude AND direction (e.g., Velocity, Force, Electric Field Intensity).
  • Unit Vector (a_A): A vector with a magnitude of 1. Formula: aA = A / |A|
2. Vector Multiplication
  • Dot Product (A · B): Yields a SCALAR. Used to find the angle between vectors or project one vector onto another.
    Rule: A · B = |A||B|cos(θ). If two vectors are orthogonal (90°), their dot product is ZERO.
  • Cross Product (A × B): Yields a VECTOR perpendicular to both A and B.
    Rule: A × B = |A||B|sin(θ) an. If two vectors are parallel (0° or 180°), their cross product is ZERO.
3. The Three Coordinate Systems
  • Cartesian (x, y, z): Unit vectors ax, ay, az are constant in direction. Volume element dv = dx dy dz.
  • Cylindrical (ρ, φ, z): Unit vectors aρ, aφ change direction based on position. Volume element dv = ρ dρ dφ dz.
  • Spherical (r, θ, φ): Unit vectors ar, aθ, aφ change direction. Volume element dv = r2 sinθ dr dθ dφ.
⚠ O'YEMI'S TRANSFORMATION TRAPS:
When converting a point from Cartesian (x,y,z) to Cylindrical (ρ,φ,z):
- ρ = √(x2 + y2)
- φ = tan-1(y/x) (Careful with the quadrant! If x is negative, add 180°).
- z = z (It remains exactly the same!).
